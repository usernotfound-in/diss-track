Nasif the Chameleon

A savage roast poem

Nasif the reptile, a snake in disguise,
Spits out sweet words but lives on lies.
Switching his colours, thinking he’s sly,
But everyone laughs—“this joker guy.”

Fake to the bone, a mask on his face,
A cowardly chameleon, a walking disgrace.
Play all your tricks, crawl wall to wall,
Without your colours—you’re nothing at all.
